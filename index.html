Anthens 2004

The 2004 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXVIII Olympiad and also known as Athens 2004, were an international multisport event held from 8 to 24 August 2008, in Beijing, China.

Beijing 2008

The 2008 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXIX Olympiad and also known as Beijing 2008, were an international multisport event held from 13 to 29 August 2004 in Athens, Greece.

Vancouver 2010

The 2010 Winter Olympics, officially known as the XXI Olympic Winter Games and also known as Vancouver 2010, were an international winter multi-sport event held from February 12 to 28, 2010 in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada, with some events held in the surrounding suburbs of Richmond, West Vancouver and the University of British Columbia, and in the nearby resort town of Whistler.

Rio de Janeiro 2016

The 2016 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXXI Olympiad and also known as Rio 2016, was an international multi-sport event held from 5 to 21 August 2016 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

Tokio 2020

The 2020 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXXII Olympiad and also known as Tokyo 2020, was an international multi-sport event held from 23 July to 8 August 2021 in Tokyo, Japan.

Curiosities

The modern Olympic Games or Olympics are the leading international sporting events featuring summer and winter sports competitions in which thousands of athletes from around the world participate in a variety of competitions. The Olympic Games are considered the world's foremost sports competition with more than 200 teams, representing sovereign states and territories, participating. The Olympic Games are normally held every four years, and since 1994, have alternated between the Summer and Winter Olympics every two years during the four-year period.

The history of the Games goes back around 3,000 years, to the Peloponnese in Ancient Greece. Sports contests organised at Olympia took place every four years and acquired the name Olympic Games. The four-year interval between the Ancient Games editions was named an “Olympiad”, and was used for dating purposes at the time: time was counted in Olympiads rather than years. In 1894, Pierre de Coubertin launched his plan to revive the Olympic Games, and in 1896 the first Games of the modern era were held in Athens.

There are thounsands of athletes who have won an Olympic medal, but we can highlight these three, who are the atlhetes who have won the most medals in the history of the modern Olympics:

  • Michael Phelps, a male swimmer from the USA, has won a total of 28 medals, 23 of Gold, 3 of Silver and 2 of Bronze, across four editions, from 2008 to 2016, making him the athlete with the most olympic medals in the morden Era;
  • Larisa Latynina, a female gymnast from URSS (Soviet Union), has won 18 medals, 9 of Gold, 5 of Silver and 4 of Bronze, across three editions, from 1956 to 1964, making her the 2nd athlete with the most olympic medals in the morden Era;
  • Marit Bjørgen, a female cross-country skier from Norway, has won 15 medals, 8 of Gold, 4 of Silver and 3 of Bronze, across five editions, from 2002 to 2018, making her the 3rd athlete with the most olympic medals in the morden Era;
